Miyamoto Musashi

宮本武蔵

“Aspire to be like Mt. Fuji, with such a broad and solid foundation that the strongest earthquake cannot move you, and so tall that the greatest enterprises of common men seem insignificant from your lofty perspective. With your mind as high as Mt Fuji you can see all things clearly. And you can see all the forces that shape events; not just the things happening near to you.”

  • Mushashi was born in Miyamoto village, in Yoshino district, Japan
  • He was a ronin, a wandering Samurai determined to live in harmony with the universe
  • His first student was a boy named Jotaro
  • He fought in the battle of Sekigahara and was shot twice but survived
  • He is known for having fought and killed over 60 people on his travels around Japan
  • There is a great novel about him named "Musashi"
  • The novel is nearly 1000 pages and was the topic of conversation on a podcast with Jocko Willink and Tim Ferriss
  • Musashi is also known for having written The Book of Five Rings
